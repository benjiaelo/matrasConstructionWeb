Geotechnical Investigation reports

MATRAS provides geotechnical investigation to obtain information on the general subsurface soil, bedrock and. subsurface exploration. Our report summarises key information about the ground conditions across a proposed development area, and commonly includes design advice and recommendations relating to those conditions. MATRAS geotechnical report typically presents factual and/or interpreted information which serves as a basis for the civil and structural design of a development.

In particular, we highlight any potentially problematic conditions, so these are addressed early in the design of the proposed structure or otherwise anticipated for mitigation during the construction. MATRAS Geotechnical reports are specific to each site, project and client and therefore have different objectives depending on the stakeholder. They include

  • General Report
  • Geotechnical Data (factual) Report
  • Geotechnical interpretative report

Geodetic Engineering/Surveying

MATRAS Geoinformation and geodetic engineering encompass a group of spatial data technologies and theory that involve one or more areas of research using photogrammetry, image understanding, mapping/geographic information science, satellite geodesy, remote sensing, real time mapping, and image processing.

At MATRAS, we emply field traditional geomatics engineering disciplines of data analysis, photogrammetry, global positioning systems (GPS) technologies, and surveying, as well as emerging areas, such as hyperspectral imaging, sensor networks, computer vision, and real-time navigation and timing. We cover:

  • Localization and Navigation with GPS
  • Cooperative Navigation
  • Safety and Security
  • Hyperspectral Imaging
